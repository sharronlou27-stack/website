Tips and Settings for Better Concert Photography

I want to discuss the best settings and techniques for concert photography. Most people who get into concert photography do so because they have a passion for music, photography, and a love of the music scene. If this sounds like you, you are already halfway there to becoming a great music photographer — but it also takes time and practice.

Low light isn’t quite the problem it once was. Gigs can be dark, but modern cameras are excellent at producing good results at high ISO. Shoot RAW! Learn manual mode to truly control your camera for consistent exposure.

Key Tips

  • Camera Settings
    • Use a fast shutter speed to freeze motion (1/200 sec or faster)
    • Push ISO as needed — sharp and slightly noisy is better than blurry
  • Technique
    • Be patient and wait for the right moment
    • Don't be afraid to move around the venue and capture a variety of angles
